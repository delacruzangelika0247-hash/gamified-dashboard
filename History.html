Philippine National Heroes

Jose Rizal

Author of "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo", Rizal fought for reforms and inspired the revolution through his writings.

Andres Bonifacio

Founder of the Katipunan, led the revolution against Spain, known as the "Father of the Philippine Revolution".

Emilio Aguinaldo

First President of the Philippines, declared independence from Spain in 1898, led revolutionary efforts.

Apolinario Mabini

"Brains of the Revolution", political adviser to Aguinaldo despite being paralyzed.

Melchora Aquino

"Tandang Sora", supported Katipunan by providing food, shelter, and medical aid to revolutionaries.

Marcelo H. del Pilar

Journalist and reformist, edited La Solidaridad to fight Spanish oppression through writing.

Lapu-Lapu

Chieftain of Mactan, resisted Magellan and defended his people against Spanish colonization.

Juan Luna

Painter and revolutionary, inspired nationalism through his artworks and reformist support.

Gabriela Silang

Revolutionary leader, fought against Spanish colonizers after the death of her husband Diego Silang.

Diego Silang

Leader of the Ilocano revolt against Spanish rule, advocated for independence and reforms.

Antonio Luna

Brilliant general of the Philippine Revolution, known for his strict discipline and military tactics.

Gregorio del Pilar

Young general, famous for his heroics in the Battle of Tirad Pass.

Josefa Llanes Escoda

Founder of the Girl Scouts of the Philippines, fought for women's rights and humanitarian causes.

Francisco Balagtas

Famous poet, author of "Florante at Laura", inspired patriotism through literature.

Manuel L. Quezon

Second President of the Philippines, known as the "Father of the National Language".

Jose Abad Santos

Supreme Court Chief Justice, martyred during WWII for refusing to cooperate with Japanese forces.

<3>Vincent Lim

First Filipino general in the Philippine Army, fought against Japanese invasion in WWII.

Sultan Dipatuan Kudarat

A leader who fought against Spanish colonization.

Emilio Jacinto

"Brains of the Katipunan", wrote laws and guided the organization during the revolution.

Evolution of the Philippine Flag

"Ang watawat ng Pilipinas ay dumaan sa ilang pagbabago mula 1897 hanggang naging opisyal na pambansang sagisag."

Why We Attend the Philippine Flag Ceremony

We attend the Philippine Flag Ceremony to show patriotism, honor national heroes, remember the country's history and struggles, and unite as Filipinos under shared values like love for country, justice, and freedom. It's a moment to express gratitude for independence, respect the flag as a symbol of Filipino identity, and renew commitment to nation-building, justice, and peace.

Sino ang Gumawa ng Watawat ng Pilipinas

Ang watawat ay unang naisip gawin ni Emilio Aguinaldo. Si Marcela Agoncillo, ang kanyang anak na si Lorenza, at ang pamangkin ni Jose Rizal na si Josefina Herbosa de Natividad ang nagtahi ng unang bandila sa Hong Kong. (Sa ibang aklat, ang pangalan ng pamangkin ay Delfina Herbosa de Natividad.)

Kahulugan ng Watawat ng Pilipinas

Ang kabuluhan ng mga kulay na pula, puti at asul ay ang mga sumusunod: Ang puting tatsulok ay sumasagisag sa pagkakapantay-pantay at kapatiran; asul para sa kalayaan, katotohanan, at katarungan; at pula para sa kabayanihan at kagitingan.
